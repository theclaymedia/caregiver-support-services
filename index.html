The Challenge

Understanding Turnover: A Training Perspective

Caregiver turnover is one of the costliest challenges facing home-care agencies, long-term care communities, and HCBS providers. High turnover disrupts continuity of care, increases burnout, and drives up recruitment and overtime costs.

Onboarding

The onboarding process for caregivers is often inadequate due to the absence of comprehensive skills training, leaving new caregivers underprepared for their responsibilities.

Burnout

Caregivers face significant emotional and physical demands. Over time, these pressures lead to burnout, the primary factor contributing to high turnover rates in caregiving.

Career Pathways

Caregivers who excel often reach a plateau due to the lack of clearly defined pathways for professional growth and advancement within the caregiving field.

Shortages

Both rural and urban areas face difficulties maintaining sufficient workforce levels, with smaller labor pools and high competition among healthcare providers.

Turnover

Annual turnover rates among CNAs, home health and personal care aides are extremely high, creating significant organizational challenges.

Without Training: 81%-121%
With Training: 35%

Training

Turnover should be recognized as a consequence of inadequate training and development. Turnover isn't a staffing issue -- it's a training issue.
